Smart MRV Platform

Designing a sustainability monitoring platform that transforms complex environmental data — carbon tracking, emissions reporting, and regulatory compliance — into clear, actionable intelligence for government and enterprise stakeholders.

Lead UX Designer I Spatial Techno Solutions Jun–Sep 2025 Sustainability / GovTech Landing Page + Dashboard

Context

Smart MRV (Measurement, Reporting & Verification) is a platform built for government agencies and enterprises to monitor greenhouse gas emissions, track environmental KPIs, and generate compliance reports aligned with international sustainability frameworks.

The platform has two core surfaces: a public-facing landing page that sells the product to decision-makers, and an internal dashboard application used by environmental officers, data analysts, and compliance teams on a daily basis.

I was brought in to design both — from scratch.

The Problem

Two distinct problems with one shared root cause: the product had powerful capabilities but no clear way to communicate or surface them.

Landing Page

  • Read like a technical specification — listed features without explaining value
  • High bounce rate. Qualified leads weren't converting because they couldn't understand what the product actually did for them
  • Two audiences (technical buyers vs. executive decision-makers) with different motivations, being served identical messaging

Dashboard Application

  • Data was scattered across disconnected views — emissions, energy, compliance each lived in separate silos
  • No clear hierarchy. Users couldn't tell what was urgent vs. informational
  • Reporting workflow required 12+ clicks and navigating between 3 different screens to generate a single compliance report
  • Zero onboarding. New users were dropped into a full-density dashboard with no guidance

Landing page hero section — outcome-first messaging with visual proof

Research & Discovery

Before opening Figma, I spent the first two weeks in research mode:

  • Competitive audit — Analyzed 8 sustainability platforms (Persefoni, Watershed, Sphera, Ecoinvent) to map positioning patterns, feature framing, and dashboard architectures
  • Stakeholder interviews — Spoke with 4 environmental officers and 2 compliance managers to understand their daily workflows, pain points, and what "actionable" actually means in their context
  • Decision journey mapping — Documented the buyer journey from awareness to conversion, identifying 5 key decision points and the objections at each stage
  • Data audit — Catalogued every data type the platform tracks (Scope 1/2/3 emissions, energy consumption, waste metrics, water usage, compliance status) to understand the information architecture requirements

Key insight: Environmental officers don't want more data — they want to know "Am I on track?" and "What needs my attention right now?" Every design decision after this was filtered through those two questions.

Design Process

Landing Page Strategy

Restructured the page around a narrative arc: "See the problem → Understand the solution → Trust the proof." Every section answers a specific objection in the buying process.

  • Hero communicates value, not features — "Track, reduce, and report emissions with confidence"
  • Visual proof sections using real dashboard screenshots instead of abstract illustrations
  • Dual-track CTA hierarchy: primary conversion for executives, secondary path for technical evaluation
  • Social proof integrated naturally — compliance certifications and measurable outcomes

Dashboard Architecture

The dashboard redesign centered on three principles: progressive disclosure, status-first hierarchy, and workflow continuity.

  • Overview-first — A single "command center" view showing aggregate emissions status, compliance health, and anomalies requiring attention
  • Drill-down pattern — Click any metric tile to dive into detailed breakdowns by source, facility, or time period — no page navigation required
  • Reporting wizard — Reduced the compliance report flow from 12+ clicks across 3 screens to a guided 4-step wizard within a single modal
  • Status indicators — Color-coded traffic light system for compliance (green/amber/red) visible at every level of the hierarchy

Solution

The final platform ships as two connected experiences that share a unified design language:

Landing Page

A conversion-optimized page that leads with outcomes. The hero immediately answers "What does this do?" with "Track, reduce, and report emissions with confidence." Below: interactive feature previews, data-driven trust sections, and a two-track CTA system that routes executives to a demo and technical buyers to documentation.

Dashboard Application

A real-time monitoring platform built for daily use by environmental teams. Key surfaces:

  • Overview Dashboard — Aggregate KPIs (total emissions, energy usage, compliance score) with trend indicators and anomaly alerts
  • Emissions Explorer — Scope 1/2/3 breakdown with filterable charts by facility, time range, and emission source
  • Compliance Center — Status tracker for regulatory requirements with deadline alerts and automated report generation
  • Reporting Module — One-click generation of compliance reports in formats required by UAE regulations and international frameworks

Key Decision

The most consequential decision was replacing the existing free-form data entry for compliance reports with a guided 4-step wizard. The team initially resisted — they felt a wizard was "too simple" for sophisticated compliance officers.

I ran a task completion test with 3 compliance managers using both approaches. With free-form entry, the average completion time was 8+ minutes with 2 errors per report. With the wizard, it dropped to under 3 minutes with zero errors. The data ended the debate.

The wizard wasn't a UX opinion — it was a measured response to observed failure patterns.

Constraint

The platform had to support both international reporting frameworks (GHG Protocol, IPCC guidelines) and UAE-specific regulatory requirements simultaneously. These frameworks use different calculation methodologies, different emission categories, and different reporting formats.

Rather than forcing users to choose a framework upfront, I designed the data input to be framework-agnostic — users enter raw operational data (fuel consumption, electricity usage, waste volumes) and the system generates reports in whichever format is required. This meant the UX stays simple while the complexity lives in the backend calculation engine.

What I Learned

Sustainability platforms are uniquely challenging because the data is inherently complex, emotionally charged, and regulatory-driven. Simplifying without dumbing down requires deep domain empathy — understanding not just what users do, but what keeps them awake at night.

The reporting wizard was the highest-impact design decision. It wasn't flashy or visually impressive — it was a boring, methodical reduction of friction. But it changed how the compliance team felt about the tool. They went from dreading month-end reporting to having confidence in a 4-step process.

The best enterprise UX isn't about making things look good — it's about making people feel competent. When a compliance officer generates a report without asking for help, that's the design working.
