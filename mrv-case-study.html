Smart MRV Platform

Smart MRV is a sustainability monitoring platform for tracking carbon emissions, energy usage, and environmental compliance. The challenge was translating deeply technical environmental data into a landing experience that could convince both technical buyers and executive decision-makers in under 60 seconds.

UX Designer I Spatial Techno Solutions Jun 2025–Sep 2025 3 months Figma Sustainability / GovTech Owned landing page UX and visual design

The Problem

The existing landing page read like a technical specification document. It listed features — "real-time emissions tracking," "automated compliance reporting," "IoT sensor integration" — without ever explaining why someone should care. The bounce rate was high, and qualified leads weren't converting because they couldn't understand what the product actually did for them.

The deeper issue: sustainability platforms serve two audiences with fundamentally different motivations. Technical users want depth and credibility. Executives want ROI and risk reduction. The page was speaking to neither effectively.

How I Approached It

  • Analyzed competitor landing pages across 8 sustainability platforms to identify messaging patterns
  • Mapped the decision journey — who visits, what questions they have, what makes them leave
  • Rewrote the content hierarchy: outcome first, feature second, technical detail on demand
  • Designed a visual narrative that uses data visualization as a storytelling device, not decoration

Design Direction

I restructured the page around a single narrative arc: "See the problem → Understand the solution → Trust the proof." Every section answers a specific objection in the buying process.

  • Hero that communicates value, not features — "Track, reduce, and report emissions with confidence"
  • Visual proof sections using real dashboard screenshots instead of abstract illustrations
  • Social proof integrated naturally — client logos, compliance certifications, measurable outcomes
  • Clear CTA hierarchy — primary conversion path for executives, secondary for technical evaluation

Impact

value proposition in 5 seconds

clear CTA hierarchy

What I Learned

Landing page design for enterprise products isn't about being clever — it's about removing every reason someone has to leave. The best conversion optimization I did wasn't a button color or headline test. It was restructuring the information architecture so that each scroll answered the next logical question a buyer would have. When the page thinks the way the buyer thinks, you don't need to "sell" — you just need to not get in the way.
