The Problem Nobody Wanted to Own

Sustainability reporting is mandatory for UAE organizations. But the tools available felt like they were designed to punish the people using them.

  • 8+ minutes per report using free-form data entry with no guidance, no validation, no feedback
  • Scattered data across spreadsheets, emails, and paper records with no centralized tracking
  • Framework confusion — officers had to understand both GHG Protocol and UAE-specific regulations to file correctly
  • Zero visibility on emissions trends — leadership couldn't see if sustainability initiatives were actually working

The compliance officer I interviewed summed it up: "I spend more time figuring out the form than thinking about the data." That sentence became our north star.

Understanding the Workflow

Before designing anything, I mapped the existing reporting process step by step.

  • Shadowed 3 compliance officers through complete reporting cycles
  • Documented every point where they paused, backtracked, or asked for help
  • Identified that 8 of 12 steps were unnecessary — they existed because the old tool lacked validation
  • Benchmarked against carbon accounting tools like Watershed and Persefoni for UX patterns

Key insight: officers weren't confused about sustainability — they were confused about the tool. The data they needed to enter was straightforward. The interface made it feel complex.

What I Designed

A unified platform covering the full sustainability workflow — from raw data collection to regulatory-ready reports — with a guided experience that builds confidence instead of anxiety.

4-Step Report Wizard

Replaced the 12-step freeform process with a guided flow. Each step validates in real-time — officers know immediately if something's wrong, not after submitting.

Emissions Dashboard

Visual tracking of Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions with trend lines and reduction targets. Leadership finally sees the impact of sustainability initiatives at a glance.

Framework-Agnostic Input

Enter raw operational data — fuel, electricity, waste volumes. The system translates to any reporting format: GHG Protocol, IPCC, or UAE regulatory. One input, many outputs.

Product Landing Page

Designed the marketing site alongside the app — one cohesive brand experience from first impression to daily use.

Design Decisions That Mattered

Wizard over free-form

Tested both approaches with real officers. The wizard reduced completion time from 8+ minutes to under 3 — with zero data entry errors. The constraint of "one thing at a time" removed paralysis.

Framework-agnostic input

Officers enter operational data, not framework-specific values. The system handles translation. This was the single decision that eliminated the most confusion — people know their fuel consumption, they don't know their Scope 1 categorization.

Progressive complexity

Basic users see summary dashboards. Power users drill into facility-level data and custom date ranges. Same data, appropriate depth. Nobody sees more than they need.

Inline validation over batch errors

The old system showed errors after submission — a wall of red text. The new wizard validates each field as you go. Errors are caught when they're easy to fix, not after 8 minutes of work.

The Core Experience

Data Entry That Guides You

Users enter raw operational data — fuel consumption, electricity usage, waste volumes — through a step-by-step wizard with inline validation. No formulas, no frameworks. Each step tells you exactly what's needed and why.

Dashboard That Tells a Story

Live emissions overview with breakdowns by scope, facility, and time period. Progress tracking against reduction targets with visual indicators that show whether initiatives are actually working — not just whether reports are filed.

One-Click Compliance

Report generation in multiple formats from a single data source. Enter data once, export in GHG Protocol, IPCC, or UAE regulatory format. The system handles the framework translation that used to consume hours.

What I Learned

The biggest takeaway: enterprise UX isn't about simplifying the data — it's about simplifying the decision. Compliance officers have the knowledge. They don't need the tool to teach them about emissions. They need the tool to stop getting in their way.

When a compliance officer generates a report without asking for help, without Googling a framework standard, without calling IT — that's the design working. The best enterprise UX is invisible competence.

If I could extend this project, I'd add collaborative features — sustainability reporting often involves multiple departments contributing data, and that handoff is the next friction point to solve.
